Fidelity Criterion (Theorem 22)

Set the accepted "distortion" level (N). As you allow more mean square error, the required transmission rate plummets.
Allowed Error (N) 10 W
R = W1 log2 (Q / N)
R = 1000 · log2 (100 / 10)
R = 3321.9 bits/sec

"Any evaluation of fidelity must correspond mathematically to an operation applied to P(x,y)... The rate for a white noise source of power Q and band W1 relative to an R.M.S. measure of fidelity is R = W1 log (Q/N)."

This established the theoretical foundation for all modern lossy compression algorithms (like JPEG and MP3), proving exactly how much data can be discarded for a given tolerance of distortion.
