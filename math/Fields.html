вЪ° 1. The Closed Circuit Axioms

вЦЉ

A field $(F,+,\cdot)$ is a closed, lossless circuit: every signal has a return path, every gain has an inverse attenuation. Elements are signals; operations are network primitives. Six laws guarantee the circuit never gets stuck:

  • Assoc.Node ordering in superposition is irrelevant: $(a+b)+c = a+(b+c)$, $(ab)c = a(bc)$. Re-routing signal paths doesn't change the net output.
  • Comm.Ports are symmetric: $a+b=b+a$, $ab=ba$. No preferred signal direction вАФ the network is reciprocal.
  • IdentityGround $\mathbf{0}$ (zero signal) and unity buffer $\mathbf{1}$ (√Ч1 gain): $a+0=a$, $a{\cdot}1=a$. The two transparent elements вАФ one adds nothing, one multiplies nothing.
  • Add. Inv.Every signal $a$ has an anti-phase signal $-a$ that cancels by superposition: $a+(-a)=0$. Guaranteed destructive interference back to ground.
  • Mult. Inv.Every non-zero signal $a$ has a reciprocal gain stage $a^{-1}$: $a\cdot a^{-1}=1$. No irreversible attenuation except into ground (0). Defines division.
  • Distrib.A gain stage after a summing junction equals parallel gain stages before it: $a(b+c)=ab+ac$. Amplification distributes over superposition вАФ the core linearity law.

A field is also a commutative ring with $0\neq1$ in which every nonzero element is a unit. Integers $\mathbb{Z}$ fail: $2^{-1}\notin\mathbb{Z}$ вАФ the √Ч2 gain stage has no matching √Ч¬љ stage in the integer network. A division ring drops commutativity вАФ the quaternions $\mathbb{H}$ (where $ij=k\neq ji=-k$) route signals through ordered 3D gain stages. Wedderburn's theorem: every finite division ring is commutative (hence a field).

вђ° 2. Number System Hierarchy Examples

вЦЉ

Rationals $\mathbb{Q}$ вАФ The prototype circuit. Every fraction $a/b$ is a precise voltage divider ratio. The anti-phase signal is $-a/b$; the reciprocal gain stage is $b/a$. Superposition distributes over gain: $\frac{a}{b}(\frac{c}{d}+\frac{e}{f}) = \frac{ac}{bd}+\frac{ae}{bf}$. Prime network of characteristic 0 вАФ the clock never resets.

Reals $\mathbb{R}$ вАФ The continuous-bandwidth completion of $\mathbb{Q}$: every Cauchy gap filled, every irrational frequency admitted. The unique gapless ordered field вАФ no measurement resolution limit. Still band-limited: $x^2+1=0$ has no real-valued solution; the $90¬∞$ phase-shift network has no real fixed point.

Complex $\mathbb{C}$ вАФ The full phasor plane. Adjoin $i$ ($i^2=-1$, a pure $90¬∞$ phase rotator) to $\mathbb{R}$. Elements $a+bi$ are phasors: $|Z|$ is amplitude, $\arg(Z)$ is phase. Multiplication = gain staging + phase rotation simultaneously. Algebraically closed: every polynomial network equation has a phasor solution.

Constructible Numbers вАФ The sub-circuit reachable from $\{0,1\}$ using only $+,-,\times,\div,\sqrt{\phantom x}$. Each extension doubles the available frequency resolution (degree a power of 2). Trisection requires a cube-root stage (degree 3, never a power of 2) вАФ outside the constructible network.

вђЖ 3. Extension Towers Degree & Algebra

вЦЉ

A field extension $F/E$ is a circuit layer stacked on a base network: new independent signal dimensions added on top. The degree $[F:E]=\dim_E F$ counts the number of independent signal channels the new layer introduces.

Tower Law (cascaded stages multiply): $[G:E]=[G:F]\cdot[F:E]$. Like cascaded amplifier stages вАФ total gain is the product of individual stage gains.

Algebraic elements satisfy a polynomial equation over the base вАФ they are resonant frequencies of a filter defined over $E$. Transcendental elements ($\pi$, $e$) are free carrier frequencies with no polynomial resonance condition. Adjoining an algebraic $x$ of minimal degree $n$: $$E(x)\cong E[X]/(f(X)),\quad [E(x):E]=n,\quad\text{signals: }\textstyle\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}a_k x^k$$

Building fields from rings вАФ two circuit techniques:
(1) Field of fractions: add a reciprocal channel for every non-zero element of ring $R$. $Q(\mathbb{Z})=\mathbb{Q}$ вАФ the integers upgraded to a full voltage-divider network.
(2) Residue field: short-circuit a maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ to zero вАФ collapse an entire equivalence class of signals to ground. $\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}=\mathbb{F}_p$. Adjoining a root of irreducible $f$: $E[X]/(f(X))$.

Algebraic closure $\bar{F}$ вАФ the fully terminated network: every polynomial filter has at least one resonant frequency inside $\bar{F}$. $\bar{\mathbb{R}}=\mathbb{C}$ вАФ adding the $90¬∞$ phase rotator $i$ completes $\mathbb{R}$ into a closed spectral space. Every field has a unique algebraic closure up to isomorphism.

Transcendence basis вАФ the minimal set of free carrier frequencies $S\subset F$ from which the rest of $F$ is algebraic (resonant). Every extension splits: a purely transcendental stage (free carriers) followed by an algebraic stage (resonances over those carriers).

вЯ≥ 4. Galois Theory Symmetry

вЦЉ

A Galois extension $F/E$ is a fully symmetric circuit: every valid rewiring of signal channels that leaves the base network $E$ fixed is a symmetry. The Galois group $\text{Gal}(F/E)$ is the complete set of these channel-permutation symmetries вАФ the automorphisms that look identical from $E$'s perspective.

Fundamental Theorem (the master circuit diagram): $$\bigl\{\text{subgroups of }\text{Gal}(F/E)\bigr\}\xleftrightarrow{\ \ 1{:}1\ \ }\bigl\{\text{intermediate fields }E\!\subset\! K\!\subset\! F\bigr\}$$ Symmetry sub-networks $\leftrightarrow$ intermediate circuit layers. The degree $[F:K]$ equals the order of the corresponding subgroup.

Solvability by radicals: Resonant frequencies of $f$ can be expressed with $+,-,\times,\div,\sqrt[n]{\phantom x}$ iff $\text{Gal}(F/E)$ is solvable вАФ decomposable into cascaded cyclic symmetry stages. $S_n$ for $n\geq5$ is not solvable: its symmetry cannot be unwound stage by stage. This is why the general quintic circuit has no closed-form frequency formula вАФ AbelвАУRuffini.

Trisection impossibility: $\cos(20¬∞)$ is a root of $8x^3-6x-1$ вАФ irreducible of degree 3 over $\mathbb{Q}$. Constructible extensions have degree $2^k$. Since $3\nmid 2^k$, trisection is geometrically impossible.

Cyclotomic fields $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)$: adjoining a primitive $n$th root of unity. $\text{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)/\mathbb{Q})\cong(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times$, order $\varphi(n)$. Kummer used these to prove Fermat's Last Theorem for regular prime exponents.

вђ° 5. Finite Fields $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}$ Galois Fields

вЦЉ

A finite field $GF(q)$ has exactly $q=p^n$ elements вАФ a digital register with $q$ discrete states, wrapping perfectly. It is the splitting field of $x^q-x$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$: every signal satisfies $a^q=a$ (every state is a fixed point of the $q$-fold clock cycle). Two finite fields of the same order are isomorphic вАФ one register architecture per size.

$\mathbb{F}_q$ is an $n$-channel bus over $\mathbb{F}_p$: $n$ independent signal lines, each carrying a $p$-state value. The multiplicative group $\mathbb{F}_q^\times$ is a cyclic shift register of period $q-1$ вАФ a single generator drives every non-zero state.

$\mathbb{F}_4 = \{O,I,A,B\}$ вАФ char 2, min-poly $X^2+X+1$ for $A$. $\{O,I\}=\mathbb{F}_2$ is a subfield:

вЖЇ 6. Characteristic & Frobenius Resonance

вЦЉ

The characteristic of $F$ is its clock overflow frequency вАФ the smallest prime $p>0$ such that $\underbrace{1+\cdots+1}_{p}=0$: the accumulator wraps back to zero after $p$ clock ticks. Char $0$ means the accumulator never overflows вАФ infinite dynamic range. Characteristic is always 0 or prime (a composite wrap period would create zero-divisors, breaking the invertibility of the network).

The Frobenius endomorphism $\phi:x\mapsto x^p$ is the canonical clock-cycle automorphism in char $p$ вАФ one full wrap of the register. It is a field homomorphism: $(a+b)^p=a^p+b^p$ because in char $p$, all cross-terms $\binom{p}{k}$ vanish (they are divisible by $p$, hence zero). Superposition commutes with the clock cycle.

The prime field is the base clock domain вАФ $\mathbb{F}_p$ in char $p$, or $\mathbb{Q}$ in char 0. It is the irreducible substrate every signal in $F$ lives over, and cannot be further sub-divided.

In char $p$: $p\cdot a=0$ for all $a$ вАФ every signal is $p$-periodic. The Frobenius twists the circuit in ways that have no char-0 analogue; it is the reason finite-field arithmetic is so structurally different from integer arithmetic.

вЗМ 7. Ordered & Complete Fields Measurement

вЦЉ

An ordered field carries a total ordering $\geq$ compatible with $+$ and $\cdot$ вАФ a unipolar signal domain where every value has a definite polarity. $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{Q}$ are ordered; $\mathbb{C}$ and $\mathbb{F}_p$ are not (no consistent notion of "positive phasor" or "positive modular residue").

ArtinвАУSchreier: $F$ can be ordered iff $-1$ is not a sum of squares вАФ the $180¬∞$ phase flip cannot be achieved by superimposing in-phase signals. Equivalently: no combination of positive-power signals sums to a negative-power signal.

An Archimedean field has no DC offset that exceeds all finite multiples of the unit вАФ no runaway bias rail, no infinitely large or infinitely small signal level. Every signal is bounded within the finite-gain network.

A Dedekind-complete ordered field has no missing frequency: every bounded set of signal values has a least upper bound within the field вАФ no spectral gap. $\mathbb{R}$ is the unique such field. This completeness directly yields the intermediate value theorem, extreme value theorem, and Cauchy convergence.

The hyperreals $\mathbb{R}^*$ admit infinitesimal signal levels $\varepsilon>0$ smaller than any rational, and infinite bias levels $H$ larger than any integer вАФ a non-Archimedean network with unbounded dynamic range in both directions. Foundation of non-standard analysis.

Archimedean: $\forall x\in\mathbb{R},\;\exists n\in\mathbb{N}: n>x$
$\mathbb{R}$ = unique Dedekind-complete ordered field

вКХ 8. Topological & p-adic Fields Completions

вЦЉ

A topological field makes all four operations continuous вАФ a field where arbitrarily small signal perturbations produce arbitrarily small output perturbations. For metric fields, completion fills Cauchy gaps: any Cauchy sequence of signals converges to a signal in the field. Completing $\mathbb{Q}$ under the standard absolute value gives $\mathbb{R}$.

The $p$-adic valuation $v_p(x)$ is a divisibility-depth sensor: it measures the exact power of prime $p$ dividing $x$. The $p$-adic metric $|x|_p=p^{-v_p(x)}$ inverts normal intuition вАФ signals with high $p$-divisibility are "close to zero." Completing $\mathbb{Q}$ under this metric gives $\mathbb{Q}_p$: a field where the bandwidth is measured in powers of $p$, not real magnitude.

Local fields: finite extensions of $\mathbb{Q}_p$ (char 0) or of $\mathbb{F}_p((t))$ (char $p$). In both, elements are power series in a uniformizer ($p$ or $t$) with $\mathbb{F}_p$ coefficients вАФ signals expressed as base-$p$ expansions. The two types are structurally parallel: the same first-order circuit equations hold in both (AxвАУKochen), and their absolute Galois groups become isomorphic after adjoining all $p$-power roots of the uniformizer.

Adjoining all $p$-power roots of the uniformizer yields perfectoid fields. Strikingly: $\text{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}_p(p^{1/p^\infty}))\cong\text{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_p((t))(t^{1/p^\infty}))$ вАФ a glimpse of the deep Langlands correspondence between these two worlds.

Algebraic $K$-theory connects field units to topology: $K_1(F)=F^\times$, $K_2(F)$ (Matsumoto) $\cong K_2^M(F)$ (Milnor). The Norm Residue Isomorphism (Voevodsky, ~2000) bridges Milnor $K$-theory to Galois cohomology: $K_n^M(F)/p\cong H^n(F,\mu_p^{\otimes n})$.

вЪ° 9. Applications Use Cases

вЦЉ

Linear Algebra: Fields are the gain domains of vector spaces. $ax=b$ ($a\neq0$) has unique solution $x=a^{-1}b$ вАФ a single well-defined operating point. Gaussian elimination is field arithmetic on a gain matrix; the existence of bases relies entirely on multiplicative inverses. Over a ring (missing inverses), the signal routing may be degenerate вАФ no solution, or infinitely many.

Cryptography (Discrete Log): In $\mathbb{F}_q$, repeated gain-staging $a^n$ is fast to compute forward; recovering $n$ from the output (discrete log) is computationally infeasible вАФ a one-way amplifier with no inverse circuit. ECC uses point-addition on $y^2=x^3+ax+b$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$ as an even harder one-way network with smaller key sizes.

Coding Theory: Reed-Solomon codes sample a polynomial signal at $n$ distinct frequencies in $\mathbb{F}_{2^8}$. Field structure guarantees exact reconstruction: $n$ sample values uniquely recover the degree-$(n{-}1)$ source signal. Errors = extra sample points that deviate; correction = solving a linear system over the finite field.

Algebraic Geometry (Function Fields): The function field $F(X)$ is the full transfer-function algebra of variety $X$ вАФ all rational signal ratios on the geometric object. For a smooth projective curve, this spectral signature completely determines $X$. Dimension of $X$ = number of independent carrier frequencies (transcendence degree of $F(X)$).

Number Theory (Global Fields): Number fields and $\mathbb{F}_q$-function fields are "global circuits." The localвАУglobal principle probes a global circuit by measuring it at every local frequency band ($\mathbb{Q}_p$, $\mathbb{R}$) вАФ HasseвАУMinkowski does this for quadratic forms. Class field theory classifies all abelian channel extensions of a global field. KroneckerвАУWeber: $\mathbb{Q}^{ab}=\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n,n\geq2)$.

Differential Fields: Equip $F$ with a derivation $D$ satisfying $D(ab)=D(a)b+aD(b)$ вАФ a continuous rate-of-change monitor on the signal network. Differential Galois theory (PicardвАУVessiot) controls which linear ODE networks admit closed-form solutions, with algebraic symmetry groups playing the role that permutation groups play for polynomial equations.
